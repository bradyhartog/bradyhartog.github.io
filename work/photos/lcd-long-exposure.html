LCD Long Exposure

University of Utah â€” 2019

An LCD screen cycles through a wide color spectrum once per second. By capturing a long exposure image over the same interval, a unique composition is created based on the starting phase of the cycle and the motion of the screen. The result over many iterations is a multifaceted series of images in which motion and color interact in subtle and unexpected ways. From over 300 iterations, these are the 16 most compelling images of the series.
